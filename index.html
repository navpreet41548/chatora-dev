OUR STORY

ARE YOU A FOOD FREAK WAITING TO HIT SOME AUTHENTIC CUISINE?

We started the Chatora Project to take you on a journey of authentic Indian street food that will connect you to your roots and traditions. Here you will find a wide collection of Famous Street Food Delicacies from ALL over India

Chef Dhiren's love for food took him on a journey where he explored every "galli" and every "rasta" that was bustling with mouth-watering street food!

In his words, "I often remember my days back in India exploring and cooking a variety of famous food from Punjab to Delhi, all the way to South India. These trips were a feast for the senses; from the vivid colours and artistry of the streets filled with delicious flavours and aromas."

Punjab

Hira Lal Papdi Chaat

Papdi Chaat is a popular punjabi street food made with papdi (fried flour crispies), boiled chickpeas, potatoes, pakoras (fried black gram fritters) and curd with a hint of chutneys and special masala.

Ludhiyane De Tikki Chana Chaat (Pure Desi Ghee)

Spicy Aloo Tikki with crisp outside and soft inside is a real taste buds anytime. Served in traditional Punjabi Style topped with Tamarind Chutney and Chana.

Amritsari Peda Lassi

Amritsar: The City of Golden Temple is also famous for its Sweet Peda Lassi prepared with Malai Peda, yogurt and topped with green cardamom powder and nuts.

Hoshiyar Puri Jal Jeera Pani

This herbaceous "jal jeera," or an Indian summer drink best described as spiced lemonade made with Jal (water), roasted cumin seed and special herbs and masala.

Mango Shake

This invigorating Mango Smoothie is the perfect refreshment for any time of the day. Mango Milk Shake topped with dry fruits.

Delhi

Raju Special Dahi Bhalla

Dahi Bhalla is an immensely popular Indian chaat dish. This protein packed, gluten-free recipe is made from lentil fritters dunked in whipped creamy yogurt and drizzled with sweet and spicy chutney and spices and Anardana, Beet roots, ginger and nuts.

LN Special Ram Ladoo

It is classic, famous street food from Delhi, and can instantly spike up the tempo of any party that it is served in. Laddus are normally sweet in taste, but Ram Laddus are salty and sour made with moong dal and are served with green chutney and grated radish.

Aloo Chaat is a spicy and tangy dish made of crispy fried potatoes tossed in spices along with sweet and tangy mint sauces garnished with coriander.

MUMBAI

Juhu Beach Pav Bhaji

A snack with almost iconic status in the western region of India, bhel puri (or bhelpuri) is a low-fat, nutritious, and delicious food. Bhel puri is a crispy, sweet, tangy, spicy dish made with puffed rice, onions, tomatoes and assorted chutneys.

Chowpatty Bhel Puri

Pav Bhaji is a spicy mashed vegetable dish (potato, cauliflower, carrot, peas, beans, tomatoes, onion, bell pepper) served piping hot with a dollop of butter, diced red onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime with 2 pc Pav.

A popular Mumbai street food, Sev Puri is a sweet, spicy and tangy chaat. Sev Puri is made with crispy puri (whole wheat crackers) topped with onion tomato chilli. Garnished with blended spices and fresh coriander.

It is classic, famous street food from Delhi, and can instantly spike up the tempo of any party that it is served in. Laddus are normally sweet in taste, but Ram Laddus are salty and sour made with moong dal and are served with green chutney and grated radish.

Special Graduate Vada Pav

This vegan street food classic from the streets of Mumbai is a carb-lovers dream. It's a fried batter coated potato dumplings sandwiched in a pav (soft dinner rolls) served with special chutney and fried green chilli.

Dahi Gujiya is another variation to Dahi Bhalla. Specialty of these Dahi Guijiyas is that these gujiyas are stuffed with chopped nuts, raisins and topped with sweetened curd, tamarind date chutney and hint of green chutney.

Ghugni Chaat

Ghugni is made with dried yellow peas/dried green peas boiled, tempered with tomatoes, onion and spices and then garnished with radish and green chilies.

Banarasi Rabri Lassi

Banarasi lassi is very different from Punjabi lassi. While Punjabi lassi is a frothy drink with a blob of fresh white butter floating on top, banarasi lassi is more like a dessert drink.Thick and sweetened curd with a topping of malai on top and a generous blob of rabdi over it.

RAJASTHAN

Khasta kachori is a very famous and loved chaat from Rajasthan. It is served with, imli ki chutney, chopped onion, green chili, yoghurt and little masalas.

Moong Dal Halwa (Pure Desi Ghee)

Moong Dal Halwa is a Rajasthani traditional sweet made using Moong lentils, Desi ghee, and sugar. Flavoured using cardamom and loaded with dry fruits, it is a rich and addictive Halwa.
